FAQ
How much does Kintone cost in the Philippines?
Kintone starts at ₱1,000 per user per month with a 5-user minimum — so a ₱5,000/month floor. Our implementation services are priced per engagement: Starter ₱95k (single app, 10-25 users), Enterprise ₱450k+ (multi-app, 50-500 users with full AI layer), Retainer from ₱85k/month for ongoing work.
How long does Kintone implementation take?
Most first apps go live within a week. A Daikin Philippines service dispatch app originally estimated at three months shipped in days. Complex multi-app enterprise deployments typically take 2-4 weeks with our 4-step process: Discovery, Design, Build, Launch.
What makes edamame different from other Kintone partners?
We won the 2024 Cybozu Global Partner of the Year award. We also run our own firm on AI — our Digital Tom system handles inbox, pipeline, and briefings on Claude Opus 4.7 for under $20/month. The operational discipline we ship to clients is the same discipline we've applied to ourselves first.
Do you support Japanese companies in the Philippines?
Yes. Founder Tom Arai trained in Tokyo before moving to Pasig in 2018. We deliver in native-level business Japanese, English, and Filipino. Bilingual Kintone UIs are our default, not an upsell. Current Japanese enterprise clients include Daikin, AEON Fantasy, Guhring, and O.M. Manufacturing Philippines.
Can Kintone replace Odoo, Salesforce, or Airtable?
For most Philippine SMEs and mid-market companies, yes. Kintone deploys in days (vs months for Odoo), needs no developers (unlike Salesforce/Odoo), and doesn't hit the 50,000-record ceiling of Airtable. We've migrated clients off all three platforms.
Is Kintone secure for enterprise?
Yes. Kintone provides SOC 2 Type II certification, ISO 27001 compliance, AES-256 encryption, role-based access controls, audit logs, two-factor authentication, and 99.99% uptime. ORIX METRO Leasing and Finance uses Kintone on an IP-whitelisted deployment for regulated financial operations.
