💰 Why Philippine SMEs Shouldn't Use SAP

SAP is designed for enterprises with complex, multi-entity global operations. For a Philippine business with 10-200 employees, SAP creates more problems than it solves: implementation costs ₱5-20M+ (Year 1), requires 6-18 months to deploy, needs certified ABAP developers for any customization (scarce and expensive in PH), ongoing maintenance and upgrade costs are substantial, and the system's complexity often exceeds what the business actually needs.

The result? Many Philippine businesses that invest in SAP end up using only 20-30% of its features while paying for 100%.

🔄 The SAP Migration Path for Philippine Businesses

Migrating away from SAP is daunting — years of data, complex integrations, and deeply embedded processes. But staying on SAP when it no longer fits your scale or budget is worse. Philippine businesses typically migrate from SAP when: annual license costs exceed 15% of IT budget, they need more than 6 months to implement changes, or they lack local SAP consultants (Philippine SAP talent commands ₱150K-300K/month).

The practical migration approach is phased: start with non-core processes. Move project tracking, customer service, HR processes, and internal workflows to Kintone while keeping SAP for core accounting and ERP functions. Once your team is comfortable, evaluate which SAP modules can be replaced and which should remain.

edamame has guided Philippine businesses through SAP-to-Kintone transitions, migrating operational processes while maintaining SAP integration for financial modules. The key is understanding that Kintone doesn't need to replace all of SAP — it replaces the 70% of SAP that you're overpaying for.
