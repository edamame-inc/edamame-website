The Philippine Onboarding Challenge

Onboarding in the Philippines involves unique requirements: 201 file collection (TIN, SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG), DOLE reporting, probationary period tracking, and often bilingual documentation. Many companies still handle this with paper forms, Excel trackers, and email chains — leading to missing documents, delayed compliance filings, and frustrated new hires.

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents are needed for Philippine onboarding?
Standard 201 file: TIN, SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG numbers, NBI clearance, 2x2 photo, diploma copies, previous employment certificate, and a signed employment contract.
How long should onboarding take in the Philippines?
Administrative onboarding (document collection, system access) should be completed within the first week. Full cultural and role onboarding typically takes 30-90 days.
Is Sprout Solutions better than Kintone for HR?
Sprout is purpose-built for Philippine payroll and HR compliance — it excels there. Kintone is better when you need onboarding integrated with your broader business operations (project management, CRM, approvals).
Can onboarding software handle probationary periods?
Yes. Kintone can track probationary milestones with automatic reminders for 30/60/90-day evaluations and regularization dates.
What's the cheapest onboarding solution?
Zoho People at ~₱80/employee/month or GreatDay HR at ~₱100/employee/month. For a free option, you can build a basic onboarding checklist in Google Sheets or Notion.
