Best Monday.com Alternatives Philippines 2026: Powerful Tools Without the Price Tag

Looking for a Monday.com alternative in the Philippines? Compare Kintone, Asana, ClickUp, Notion, and Trello — with real pricing in pesos and honest feature comparison.

📅 2026-04-13 ⏱ 10 min read ✍️ Tom Arai, CEO — Edamame Inc.

Why Monday.com Gets Expensive Fast

Monday.com looks affordable at first — plans start around ₱500/user/month. But here's what they don't tell you upfront: there's a 3-seat minimum, the features you actually need (automations, integrations, dashboards) are locked behind Pro plans at ₱1,500+/user/month, and you're paying in USD with Philippine bank conversion fees.

For a 10-person team on Pro, you're looking at ₱15,000+/month — for what is essentially a project board with automations. Here are five alternatives that offer more flexibility.

1. Kintone — Best for Custom Workflows (from ₱1,000/user/month)

Kintone doesn't just give you project boards — it lets you build complete business applications without coding. Track projects, manage approvals, handle client data, and automate workflows all in one platform. Where Monday.com gives you a visual board, Kintone gives you a full business operating system.

Best for: Teams that need project management integrated with their actual business processes (sales, HR, operations).

2. Asana — Best Free Plan for Small Teams (Free for 15 users)

Asana's free plan supports up to 15 users with unlimited tasks and projects. The interface is clean, the mobile app is solid, and it integrates well with Google Workspace — which many Philippine businesses already use.

Best for: Small teams that need task management without the cost.

3. ClickUp — Best Feature Density (Free / from ~₱350/user/month)

ClickUp tries to be everything — project management, docs, whiteboards, goals, time tracking. The free plan is generous, and paid plans are cheaper than Monday.com. The tradeoff is a steeper learning curve.

Best for: Power users who want maximum features at minimum cost.

4. Notion — Best for Documentation + Projects (Free / from ~₱500/user/month)

Notion combines wiki, project management, and databases in a flexible workspace. Great for teams that produce a lot of documents alongside their projects. Not as strong on automations or reporting as dedicated PM tools.

Best for: Knowledge-heavy teams (agencies, consultants, research) that need docs and projects together.

5. Trello — Best for Simple Kanban (Free / from ~₱300/user/month)

Trello's Kanban boards are simple, intuitive, and free for basic use. Power-Ups add integrations and automations. It's Monday.com's original inspiration — simpler, cheaper, and sometimes that's exactly what you need.

Best for: Teams with straightforward workflows that don't need complex automations.

Which Alternative Is Right for Your Philippine Business?

If you need project management plus business operations, Kintone delivers the most value. If you need pure task management for free, Asana is hard to beat. For maximum features at minimum cost, ClickUp wins. And if simplicity is king, Trello keeps things clean.

The smartest Philippine businesses don't ask 'which project tool is best?' — they ask 'what else do we need beyond project management?' If the answer includes CRM, approvals, or custom workflows, that's where Kintone stands alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Kintone good for project management?
Yes. Kintone lets you build custom project tracking apps with Kanban views, Gantt-style timelines, and automated status updates. Many Philippine companies use it for project management integrated with their CRM and operations.
What's cheaper than Monday.com in the Philippines?
Trello and ClickUp both have generous free plans. Asana offers free for up to 15 users. Kintone at ₱1,000/user/month includes project management plus unlimited custom business apps.
Can Monday.com handle CRM?
Monday.com has a CRM product, but it's separate from the project management product and costs extra. Kintone handles both in one platform with no additional cost.
Is ClickUp reliable?
ClickUp has improved significantly in reliability since 2024. However, the sheer feature density means it can feel overwhelming. For Philippine businesses wanting simplicity, Kintone or Asana may be better choices.
Does Trello work for large teams?
Trello can work for larger teams, but it becomes harder to manage as complexity grows. For teams over 15 people with complex workflows, Kintone or Monday.com provide better structure.
