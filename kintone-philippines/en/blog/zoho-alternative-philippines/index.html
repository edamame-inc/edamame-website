Best Zoho Alternatives Philippines 2026: When You Need More Than a Suite

Outgrown Zoho or frustrated by its complexity? Compare the best Zoho alternatives for Philippine businesses — Kintone, HubSpot, Monday.com, Freshworks, and Salesforce Essentials.

📅 2026-04-13 ⏱ 10 min read ✍️ Tom Arai, CEO — Edamame Inc.

Why Companies Switch Away from Zoho

Zoho's biggest strength is also its biggest weakness: 55+ apps that promise everything. In practice, many Philippine businesses find themselves drowning in a sea of half-used tools. The integration between Zoho apps isn't always smooth, the UI feels dated compared to modern alternatives, and getting support can be frustrating when you're in the Philippine timezone.

If you're considering alternatives, here are five platforms worth evaluating — each with a different strength.

1. Kintone — Best for Custom Business Apps (from ₱1,000/user/month)

Where Zoho gives you 55 pre-built apps that you adapt to your process, Kintone lets you build exactly the apps your business needs — from scratch, without coding. Need a CRM that matches your unique sales process? A project tracker that works the way your team thinks? An approval workflow that reflects your org structure? Build it in Kintone.

Why switch from Zoho: Instead of forcing your process into Zoho's structure, build software that matches your business. Enterprise-grade security with Japanese engineering quality. Local support from Edamame Inc. in Manila.

Best for: Companies that need custom workflows, not just pre-built templates.

2. HubSpot — Best for Inbound Marketing (Free / Pro from ₱45,000+/month)

If your main frustration with Zoho is its marketing capabilities, HubSpot's inbound marketing engine is best-in-class. The free CRM is genuinely useful, and the marketing tools (landing pages, email automation, SEO) are more polished than Zoho's.

The catch: Expensive once you need Professional features. USD pricing hits Philippine businesses hard.

Best for: Marketing-first companies with budget for premium tools.

3. Monday.com — Best for Visual Project Management (~₱1,500/user/month)

If you've been using Zoho Projects and finding it clunky, Monday.com's visual interface is a breath of fresh air. Colorful boards, easy drag-and-drop, and strong automations make project tracking almost enjoyable.

Best for: Creative teams and project-heavy businesses that need visual workflows.

4. Freshworks — Best for Customer Support (from ~₱900/user/month)

Freshworks (Freshdesk + Freshsales + Freshservice) offers a cleaner, more modern alternative to Zoho's suite at similar price points. The support desk is particularly strong — better than Zoho Desk in most reviews.

Best for: Service-oriented businesses that need strong ticketing and customer support tools.

5. Salesforce Essentials — Best for Enterprise-Ready CRM (~₱1,400/user/month)

If you're outgrowing Zoho CRM and need enterprise features (advanced reporting, AI predictions, massive app ecosystem), Salesforce Essentials is the entry point to the world's largest CRM platform.

The catch: Complexity. Salesforce is powerful but requires significant setup and often consulting help. Costs escalate quickly beyond Essentials.

Best for: Fast-growing companies preparing for enterprise-scale operations.

Our Recommendation

For most Philippine businesses switching from Zoho, the choice comes down to what frustrated you. If it was rigidity — you couldn't customize things the way you needed — Kintone is the answer. If it was marketing capabilities, look at HubSpot. If it was project management, try Monday.com. And if it was customer support tools, Freshworks is strong.

What we see most often at Edamame Inc. is companies that tried Zoho, found the pre-built apps 80% right but couldn't close that last 20% gap. That's exactly where Kintone shines — you build the 100% right solution for your business.
