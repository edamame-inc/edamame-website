What a Kintone HR System Covers

1. Employee Records Management

A centralized employee database with all fields relevant to Philippine HR: full name, employee number, TIN, SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG numbers, emergency contacts, employment date, position, department, salary grade, regularization date, and employment status. Documents (contract, IDs, certificates) attached directly to the employee record.

2. Leave Management

Philippine leave types built in from day one: Vacation Leave, Sick Leave, Service Incentive Leave (SIL), Maternity Leave (105 days), Paternity Leave (7 days), Solo Parent Leave, and company-specific leaves. Employees submit leave requests through Kintone, managers approve digitally, the calendar updates automatically, and HR sees real-time headcount at any given date.

3. Attendance and Timekeeping

Log daily attendance, late arrivals, overtime requests, and schedule deviations. Connect to biometric systems via API or manage manually through the app. Automatic summaries for payroll cut-off periods.

4. Expense and Reimbursement

Employees submit expense reports with uploaded receipts directly in Kintone. Amounts auto-tabulated, routing to the appropriate approver based on amount thresholds and department. Finance sees a dashboard of pending reimbursements by cost center.

5. Performance Management

KPI tracking, quarterly review forms, goal-setting records, and 360-feedback workflows — all in Kintone, with historical data kept permanently and accessible to HR and leadership.

Guhring Philippines built exactly this. Their non-IT sales employee built a leave request app with embedded permissions so employees could file and get approvals without HR needing to manually track anything. The team calendar updates in real time.

Philippine Labor Law Compliance

Philippine HR management requires compliance with the Labor Code, DOLE regulations, SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG contributions, and BIR tax requirements. Your HR system must track Service Incentive Leave (5 days minimum after 1 year), 13th month pay calculations, and separation pay obligations.

Kintone's custom app approach means you build HR processes that match exact Philippine requirements — not a generic global HRIS that you force-fit to local laws. Track 201 file completeness, monitor probationary period milestones (Day 1, Month 3, Month 5 evaluations), manage contract renewals for fixed-term employees, and generate DOLE-required reports.

For compliance-sensitive processes like termination documentation, Kintone's process management ensures proper approval chains — HR initiates, legal reviews, department head confirms, GM approves — with full audit trails that protect the company in labor disputes.

Employee Self-Service and Mobile Access

Modern Philippine employees expect self-service HR capabilities. Kintone's mobile app enables employees to submit leave requests, view remaining leave balances, access company policies, submit expense reports with receipt photos, and complete onboarding documents — all from their smartphones.

For Philippine companies with field employees, delivery drivers, or multi-site workers, this mobile accessibility eliminates the need to visit the office for routine HR transactions. Managers approve requests from their phones, and HR staff monitor everything from a centralized dashboard.

Kintone also supports custom notification workflows — automatic reminders for expiring medical certificates, upcoming contract renewals, birthday greetings, and performance review schedules. These automated touchpoints improve employee engagement without creating additional work for HR staff.

Frequently Asked Questions

What HR system is best for Philippine companies?
For Philippine SMEs (5-100 employees), Kintone provides the best balance of flexibility and cost. Build custom HR apps for leave management, 201 file tracking, performance reviews, and onboarding — all compliant with DOLE requirements. For payroll computation and BIR tax filing, pair Kintone with a dedicated Philippine payroll system like Sprout or PayrollHero.
Is Kintone DOLE compliant for HR management?
Kintone can be configured to track all DOLE-required employment records, leave balances (SIL, vacation, sick leave per Philippine labor law), and compliance documents. However, Kintone is not payroll software — for statutory deductions (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG) and BIR tax computation, use a dedicated Philippine payroll system and connect it to Kintone via API.
How much does an HR system cost in the Philippines?
Basic HRIS solutions in the Philippines range from ₱100-500/employee/month. Enterprise HR suites (SAP SuccessFactors, Workday) cost ₱2,000-5,000/employee/month. Kintone's approach — building custom HR apps at ₱1,000/user/month for HR staff — is typically 50-70% cheaper because you pay per HR user, not per employee.
