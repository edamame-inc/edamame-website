🇵🇭 Philippine Retail Requirements

Any retail software used in the Philippines must address: BIR-accredited receipting and invoicing, VAT and non-VAT transaction handling, multi-branch inventory synchronization, Philippine payment methods (GCash, Maya, bank transfers), customer loyalty and rewards tracking, and integration with Philippine accounting software for BIR reporting.

📱 Omnichannel Retail: Online + Physical Stores

Philippine retail is rapidly shifting to omnichannel. Consumers expect to browse online, check store availability, buy via GCash or Maya, and pick up in-store or receive COD delivery. Your software stack needs to handle this seamlessly.

The practical architecture for Philippine omnichannel retail: Shopify or WooCommerce for your online storefront, a BIR-compliant POS for physical transactions, and Kintone as the operational backbone — syncing inventory between online and offline channels, managing customer data across touchpoints, and tracking orders from placement through delivery.

Kintone's REST API enables real-time synchronization between your e-commerce platform and your inventory database, ensuring that online stock levels reflect actual physical inventory across all branches. This prevents the overselling problem that plagues Philippine retailers running disconnected systems.

📦 Supplier and Procurement Management

Philippine retailers deal with a mix of local suppliers, importers, and international brands — each with different payment terms, lead times, and quality standards. Managing this manually or through spreadsheets leads to stockouts, overstocking, and missed early payment discounts.

With Kintone, you can build a complete procurement system: supplier database with performance ratings, automated purchase orders triggered by reorder points, delivery tracking with photo documentation, quality inspection checklists, and payment scheduling aligned with supplier terms. All connected to your inventory and sales data.

For retailers importing goods, Kintone tracks the entire import lifecycle — purchase order, shipping documents, customs clearance, warehouse receipt, and inventory allocation — giving you visibility into landed costs and delivery timelines that spreadsheets can't provide.

What is the best retail management system in the Philippines?

For Philippine retailers, the best approach is a modular stack: a BIR-compliant POS system (QNE for accounting-integrated POS, or Shopify POS for omnichannel) paired with Kintone for inventory management, customer CRM, supplier tracking, and multi-store operations. This gives you specialized transaction processing plus flexible operations management.

How do I track inventory across multiple branches in the Philippines?

Kintone excels at multi-branch inventory management. Set up inventory apps with location fields, inter-branch transfer workflows, reorder point alerts, and real-time stock dashboards. Staff at each branch update inventory via mobile, and headquarters sees consolidated real-time data. Many Philippine retailers manage 5-50+ locations this way.

What is the best POS system for small business in Philippines?

For Philippine small retailers, QNE offers the best BIR-compliant POS + accounting combination. For omnichannel retail (physical + online store), Shopify POS integrates well. Pair either with Kintone for customer management, inventory across locations, and operations management.

Can I manage multiple store locations with Kintone?

Yes. Kintone handles multi-location inventory tracking, inter-branch transfer management, location-specific reporting, and centralized customer database across all branches. Many Philippine retail businesses use Kintone to manage 5-50+ locations from a single platform.
