12 Best Accounting Software in Philippines 2026: BIR-Ready Comparison Guide

📅 Updated: April 4, 2026 ⏱️ 14 min read 👤 By Tom Arai, edamame

Choosing accounting software in the Philippines is different from choosing it anywhere else. You need BIR compliance, VAT computation, withholding tax handling, and the ability to generate government-mandated reports. Pick the wrong software and you're doing manual BIR submissions on top of your accounting — defeating the entire purpose. For more details, see our guide to best ERP software for Philippine businesses.

We've analyzed 12 accounting platforms based on BIR readiness, pricing in Philippine Pesos, ease of use, cloud access, and suitability for different business sizes. Whether you're a freelancer in Makati, an SME in Cebu, or a manufacturing company in Laguna, this guide covers your options.

⚠️ BIR CAS Requirement: If your business earns over ₱3 million annually, the Bureau of Internal Revenue requires you to use a registered Computerized Accounting System (CAS) with a Permit to Use (PTU). Not all software on this list is BIR CAS-accredited — we flag which ones are.

Detailed Reviews

1 QuickBooks Online

Best Overall for SMEs

QuickBooks Online remains the most widely used cloud accounting software among Philippine SMEs. Its strength is ease of use — most business owners can start invoicing and tracking expenses within the first hour, without accounting knowledge. The Philippine-specific version supports multi-currency (critical for businesses dealing in USD and JPY), connects to local banks, and integrates with over 750 third-party apps including Shopify, PayPal, and Gcash for payment tracking.

2 Xero

The platform handles unlimited users on all plans (a rare advantage), supports both cash and accrual accounting (IFRS-compliant for PH businesses), and has deep inventory management. Xero recently launched JAX, an AI financial assistant that automates invoicing and provides real-time business insights.

3 QNE Cloud Accounting

🇵🇭 Best BIR-Native Solution

QNE is built from the ground up for Philippine businesses. It is fully BIR CAS-accredited, generates BIR forms directly (2550M, 2550Q, 2551Q, 1601C, and more), and produces the required DAT files for electronic submission. For businesses that need guaranteed BIR compliance without workarounds, QNE is the safest choice. The AI-powered cloud version launched in 2025 adds automated bank reconciliation, smart categorization, and predictive analytics.

This eliminates the reconciliation headaches that businesses face when using separate accounting and tax compliance tools.

Zoho Books offers a remarkably complete accounting feature set at the most affordable price point among established platforms. Being part of the Zoho ecosystem means you can add CRM (Zoho CRM), HR (Zoho People), and project management (Zoho Projects) without switching platforms.

6. Wave — Best Free Accounting

Wave offers genuinely free accounting software — no trial period, no user limits, no feature gates on core accounting. You get invoicing, expense tracking, bank connections, and financial reporting at zero cost. The catch: Wave makes money through payment processing (2.9% + 30¢ per transaction) and optional payroll services. For Filipino freelancers, sole proprietors, and micro-businesses earning under ₱3M (below the BIR CAS threshold), Wave is hard to beat. No BIR compliance features, but at that revenue level, manual BIR filing is manageable.

7. FreshBooks — Best for Service Businesses

FreshBooks excels at time tracking, project billing, and client invoicing — making it ideal for consultants, agencies, law firms, and creative professionals in the Philippines. Starting at around ₱500/month, it's affordable, and its invoicing interface is the most polished in the market. However, FreshBooks is not a full double-entry accounting system on its lowest plan, and it lacks BIR-specific features. Best used alongside a local accountant handling compliance.

8. Sage 50cloud — Best for Mid-Size Companies

Sage 50cloud (formerly Peachtree) is a hybrid desktop-cloud system used by many mid-size Philippine businesses, especially those with complex inventory, multiple warehouses, and manufacturing operations. AccountingPro (Mustard Seed Systems) is the largest Sage partner in the Philippines, offering implementation, training, and BIR compliance support. Starting around ₱3,000/month, Sage is pricier but offers deeper inventory and job costing features than pure cloud alternatives.

9. ABSS (formerly MYOB) — Best Desktop Solution

ABSS is the go-to desktop accounting software for Philippine businesses that prefer a one-time purchase over monthly subscriptions. At approximately ₱15,000 for a perpetual license, it suits businesses with stable requirements that don't need cloud access. ABSS handles multi-currency, GST/VAT, inventory, and payroll. Not cloud-native, but reliable for businesses that want their data on local servers. BIR compliance available through local resellers.

10. AutoCount — Best for Retail + POS

AutoCount combines accounting, inventory, and point-of-sale in a single platform, making it a strong choice for Philippine retail businesses, restaurants, and trading companies. It's BIR CAS-accredited and generates the required reports for tax compliance. The built-in POS module means you don't need a separate retail system — transactions flow directly into your books. Starting around ₱2,000/month, it competes well against separate accounting + POS combinations.

11. Oracle NetSuite — Best for Enterprises

NetSuite is the enterprise cloud ERP standard, used by large Philippine corporations with multi-entity, multi-currency, and multi-country requirements. Starting at approximately ₱300,000+/year, it's not for SMEs — but for companies with ₱100M+ revenue, complex supply chains, and subsidiaries, NetSuite offers unmatched depth in financial consolidation, revenue recognition, and compliance. Fasttrack Solutions is the leading NetSuite partner in the Philippines with offices in Makati, BGC, Cebu, and Davao.

12. SAP Business One — Best for Manufacturing

SAP Business One serves mid-to-large Philippine manufacturers and distributors needing deep production planning, material requirements planning (MRP), and cost accounting. Starting around ₱250,000+/year for cloud, it includes financial management, purchasing, inventory, production, and CRM in one integrated system. SAP is BIR-compliant through Philippine implementation partners and is trusted by companies that need audit trails meeting international standards.

When a business owner says "I need accounting software," they usually mean:

  • "I'm tracking expenses in Excel and it's a mess" — That's operations, not accounting

How to Choose: Decision Framework

If you're a freelancer or sole proprietor under ₱3M revenue: Start with Wave (free) or FreshBooks (₱500/mo). You don't need BIR CAS compliance yet. Use a spreadsheet or Kintone for expense tracking and invoicing.

If you're an SME earning ₱3M-50M: You need BIR CAS compliance. Go with QNE or Juan for guaranteed BIR readiness, or QuickBooks/Xero with a local CPA handling compliance. Pair with Kintone for operational workflows (PO approvals, expense management, budget tracking). For more details, see our guide to automating financial approval workflows.

If you're a mid-size company earning ₱50M-500M: Sage 50cloud or AutoCount with a local partner. Consider adding Kintone for custom workflows that your accounting software can't handle — client billing, project tracking, inventory beyond what accounting offers.

If you're an enterprise earning ₱500M+: Oracle NetSuite or SAP Business One. At this scale, you need financial consolidation, multi-entity management, and international compliance.

Final Thoughts

The best accounting software for your Philippine business depends on two things: your BIR compliance needs and the size of your operations. For most SMEs, the winning combination isn't the most expensive all-in-one system — it's a focused accounting tool for compliance paired with a flexible platform like Kintone for everything else.

Don't pay ₱300,000/year for an ERP when ₱2,500/month for QNE + ₱5,000/month for Kintone gives you more flexibility, faster deployment, and a system your team will actually use.
