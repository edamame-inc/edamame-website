01 — Origin

The Seed of Life

One circle. Then six more at 60° intervals, each touching the center. Seven circles — the geometric seed from which everything grows.

7 circles, all radius R Centers at distance R from origin, at 60° intervals sin(60°) = √3 / 2 ≈ 0.866 This one value positions every circle, defines every intersection, and determines every bean proportion.

The Seed of Life appears in the Temple of Osiris at Abydos, in Da Vinci's notebooks, and in the molecular structure of carbon. It is the first step of the Flower of Life — the geometric pattern that encodes creation itself.

02 — Form

The Vesica Piscis

Where any two seed circles overlap, they produce an almond shape whose proportions cannot be anything other than √3 to 1. This is the bean.

Circle A at origin, Circle B at (R, 0), both radius R Intersection points: x = R / 2 y = ± R√3 / 2 Width between tips = R Height at midpoint = R × √3 Aspect ratio = √3 : 1 = 1.732 : 1 The shape is defined by two arcs. Each arc is a segment of a circle. There is nothing arbitrary here.

The vesica piscis — the womb shape between two worlds. Its ratio is locked to √3, a consequence of the 60° hexagonal grid. You cannot change it without breaking the geometry. Every edamame bean in the mark carries this proportion.

03 — Proportion

φ — The Golden Ratio

The center bean is generated from circles whose radius is φ × R. It is not "slightly bigger" — it is precisely 1.618 times the side beans in every dimension.

Side beans: circles radius R, separation R → Width = R, Height = R√3 Center bean: circles radius φR, separation φR → Width = φR, Height = φR√3 φ = (1 + √5) / 2 = 1.618034... Center / Side = φ in width, height, and diagonal Center / Side = φ² in area Same aspect ratio (√3:1). Pure scaling.

φ governs the spiral of a nautilus shell, the branching of trees, the arrangement of petals. In the edamame mark, it makes the center bean feel naturally dominant — the gold core flanked by green growth. Not by design intuition, but by mathematics.
